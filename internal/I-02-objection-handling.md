# Objection Handling Guide

*Top 20 objections you'll hear, with responses that actually work.*
*Tone: confident, not pushy. Acknowledge, reframe, prove.*

---

## PRICE OBJECTIONS

### 1. "That's too expensive"
> "I hear you. Let me ask this — how much time does [THEIR PAIN POINT] cost you per week? If it's even 10 hours, at your billing rate, you're spending more than our setup fee every month. The agent pays for itself by month 2."

If they push back harder:
> "We can start with a smaller scope — just [ONE KEY TASK] — and expand once you see results. That brings the setup down."

### 2. "Can't you do it for less?"
> "We could, but you'd get less. The setup fee covers discovery, custom configuration, testing, deployment, and handoff. We could cut corners, but you'd feel it in quality. What I can do is phase the build — start with the highest-impact piece and add the rest over time."

### 3. "We'll just hire someone instead"
> "A part-time admin in DFW runs $2,000-3,000/month. They work 8 hours, take breaks, call in sick, need training, and quit. Your agent works 24/7, never forgets a follow-up, and costs $500/month after setup. Plus it scales without hiring again."

### 4. "We need to see ROI first"
> "Totally fair. Here's what I can do — we'll define 3 measurable KPIs before we build anything. Something like: response time to leads, follow-up completion rate, appointments booked per week. We track them from day 1. If we're not moving those numbers in 30 days, we'll troubleshoot together."

## TRUST / SKEPTICISM OBJECTIONS

### 5. "We tried a chatbot before and it was terrible"
> "Yeah, most are. Here's the difference — chatbots follow a script. They break the second someone asks something unexpected. What we build is an actual AI agent that understands context, adapts, and handles real conversations. It's the difference between a phone tree and a person."

### 6. "AI makes mistakes / hallucinates"
> "It can, which is exactly why we build guardrails. Your agent has boundaries — things it handles alone, things it escalates to you. It won't make up prices, promise things you don't offer, or go rogue. We test 20 scenarios before it goes live."

### 7. "How do I know my data is safe?"
> "Your agent runs on your own infrastructure. We don't store your client data on shared servers. Everything is encrypted, access-controlled, and we follow the same security practices used by enterprise companies. I can walk you through the technical setup if you'd like."

### 8. "What if something goes wrong?"
> "Two things protect you. First, the agent has escalation rules — if it's unsure, it hands off to you or your team immediately. Second, we monitor the agent daily for the first month. If something's off, we catch it before your clients notice."

### 9. "I don't understand AI"
> "You don't need to. Think of it like hiring a really sharp virtual assistant who already knows your business. You tell them what to do, they do it. You check their work. Over time, they get better. We handle all the technical stuff."

## TIMING OBJECTIONS

### 10. "Not the right time"
> "When would be? Because here's the thing — every week without this, you're losing [QUANTIFIED PAIN: leads, hours, revenue]. We can start small and build as you're ready. But waiting means those leads keep falling through."

### 11. "We're too busy right now to set this up"
> "That's actually the best reason to do it. You're busy because you're doing work the agent should handle. Setup takes 1-2 hours of your time for the discovery call and a review session. We handle everything else."

### 12. "Let me think about it"
> "Of course. What specifically do you want to think through? If it's the price, the fit, or the timing — I might be able to help right now."

*If they can't articulate what they need to think about, they're probably not buying. Set a follow-up date and move on.*
> "Tell you what — I'll send the proposal over. Take a look, and let's reconnect [SPECIFIC DAY]. If it's not right, no hard feelings."

## COMPETITION / ALTERNATIVE OBJECTIONS

### 13. "We're already using Zapier / HubSpot / [TOOL]"
> "Those are great tools — for what they do. Zapier connects things but doesn't think. HubSpot tracks but doesn't act. Your agent actually does the work: writes the email, books the appointment, follows up, handles the back-and-forth. It's the layer on top of your existing tools that makes them useful."

### 14. "My nephew / intern / IT guy can set up ChatGPT for us"
> "They can set up ChatGPT, sure. But ChatGPT doesn't know your pricing, your processes, your clients' names, or when to escalate. What we build is a custom agent trained on your business that connects to your calendar, your CRM, and your email. That's not a ChatGPT prompt — that's an operations system."

### 15. "Why not just use [COMPETITOR]?"
> "Depends what they're offering. Most 'AI automation' companies sell chatbots — scripted, limited, breaks when things get complex. We build full-stack AI agents that operate like a team member. Happy to compare specifics — what are they proposing?"

## SCOPE / FIT OBJECTIONS

### 16. "We're too small for this"
> "Actually, small businesses get the most value. You don't have the budget for 3 more hires, but you have the work that needs them. The agent fills that gap for a fraction of the cost. Our sweet spot is 5-50 employee businesses."

### 17. "We're too big / complex for this"
> "Fair question. We handle complexity by scoping carefully. We don't try to automate everything at once. We pick the highest-impact workflow, nail it, then expand. For larger orgs, that usually means starting with one department or one process."

### 18. "My industry is different"
> "Every industry says that, and every industry has the same core problem: too much admin work, not enough time for the work that actually makes money. Whether you're in dental, property management, HVAC, or law — the pattern is the same. We customize the agent to your specific workflows."

### 19. "What if we outgrow it?"
> "That's the best-case scenario. The agent scales with you — more clients, more workflows, more automation. No new hires needed. And if your needs change, we reconfigure. That's what the monthly support covers."

### 20. "Can I talk to one of your clients?"
> "Absolutely. Let me connect you with [CLIENT NAME] — they're in [SIMILAR INDUSTRY] and have been running their agent for [X WEEKS]. They'll give you the unfiltered version."

*If we don't have a reference yet:*
> "We're just launching this practice, so I'll be honest — you'd be one of our first dedicated agent builds. The upside? You get founder-level attention, direct access to me, and we're pricing early clients aggressively because your success is our case study."

## Quick Response Framework

When an objection catches you off guard, use this pattern:

1. **Acknowledge:** "That's a fair point."
2. **Reframe:** "Here's how I think about it..."
3. **Evidence:** One specific fact, number, or comparison.
4. **Question back:** "Does that address your concern, or is there something else?"

Never argue. Never get defensive. If they're not buying, that's fine. Not every lead is a client.
