# Discovery Call Script — 30 Minutes

*For Thomas (and later, trained salespeople). Adapt naturally — this is a guide, not a teleprompter.*

---

## 1. RAPPORT (3 min)

**Goal:** Human connection. They need to like you before they'll listen.

> "Hey [NAME], thanks for making the time. Before we jump in — I looked at [COMPANY] and [GENUINE OBSERVATION]. How long have you been running things?"

Pick one genuine thing: a recent review, a cool project, how long they've been in business. Don't fake it.

Let them talk. Mirror energy. If they're casual, be casual. If they're all business, match it.

---

## 2. PAIN DISCOVERY (10 min)

**Goal:** Find the real problem. Not what they think they want — what's actually costing them.

**Open with:**
> "So what made you book this call? What's the thing that's been bugging you?"

Let them talk. Then dig deeper:

**The 5 pain probes:**

1. **The day-in-the-life question:**
   > "Walk me through a typical day. Where do things get stuck or take longer than they should?"

2. **The missed opportunity question:**
   > "What falls through the cracks? Leads that don't get followed up, appointments that get missed?"

3. **The scale question:**
   > "If you could clone yourself, what would the clone handle?"

4. **The cost question:**
   > "Roughly — what do you think this stuff is costing you? In hours, missed revenue, whatever comes to mind."

5. **The failed attempt question:**
   > "Have you tried automating any of this before? What happened?"

**Listen for these signals:**
- ⭐ Hours wasted on repetitive tasks (scheduling, follow-ups, data entry)
- ⭐ Leads falling through cracks (nobody follows up, slow response)
- ⭐ Staff doing work below their skill level
- ⭐ They've tried chatbots/Zapier and it didn't stick
- ⭐ Growing fast but ops can't keep up

**Write down exact quotes.** "We lose 3-4 leads a week because nobody calls back within an hour" is gold for the proposal.

---

## 3. CURRENT STATE (5 min)

**Goal:** Map their tech + team. This determines what we build.

> "Let me understand what you're working with right now."

**Quick inventory:**
- "What tools do you use? CRM? Calendar? Email? Anything for scheduling?"
- "How many people are on the team?"
- "Who handles [THE PAIN POINT THEY MENTIONED]?"
- "How do leads come in right now?" (website form, phone, walk-in, referral)
- "Do you have a website? Social media? Google Business profile?"

**Fill in mentally:**
- Tech sophistication: Low (paper/phone) → Medium (basic tools) → High (CRM, automation)
- Team capacity: Understaffed / right-sized / overstaffed on admin
- Decision speed: Fast (owner decides now) → Slow (committee, board, partner)

---

## 4. VISION (5 min)

**Goal:** Paint the picture. Make them feel what "fixed" looks like.

> "So here's what I'm hearing — [SUMMARIZE THEIR TOP 2-3 PAIN POINTS]. Sound right?"

Wait for confirmation. Then:

> "What if you had someone — not a chatbot, a real operator — that handled [SPECIFIC TASK] automatically? Every lead gets a response within 5 minutes. Every follow-up happens on time. Every appointment gets confirmed. And you get a summary every morning of what happened while you slept."

> "That's what we build. A dedicated AI agent for [COMPANY]. Not a generic tool — one that knows your business, your tone, your processes."

**Don't oversell.** Be honest about what it can and can't do:
- ✅ "It handles the 80% — the repetitive stuff that eats your day"
- ✅ "You stay in control — it escalates anything important to you"
- ❌ Don't say "it replaces employees" (triggers fear)
- ❌ Don't promise specific ROI numbers yet (that's for the proposal)

---

## 5. QUALIFY (5 min)

**Goal:** Confirm they're a real opportunity, not a tire-kicker.

**Budget:**
> "For context — our setups typically run $3,000-8,000 depending on complexity, with monthly support starting at $500. Does that land in the range you were thinking?"

If they flinch hard → they're not ready. Be graceful:
> "Totally understand. What I can do is send you some resources — and when the timing's right, we're here."

**Authority:**
> "Are you the one who'd make this call, or do we need to loop anyone else in?"

**Timeline:**
> "If this made sense — when would you want to get started? This week? This month?"

**Tech readiness:**
> "How comfortable is your team with new tools? We handle all the setup, but your team will interact with the agent daily."

---

## 6. NEXT STEPS (2 min)

**Goal:** Lock in the next action. Never end a call without one.

**If qualified:**
> "Here's what I'd suggest. I'll put together a proposal — specific to what we talked about. You'll see exactly what the agent would handle, how it works, and what it costs. I'll have that to you by [DATE — within 48 hours]. Sound good?"

> "One more thing — I'll send a quick recap of what we discussed today. If I missed anything, just let me know."

**If not ready:**
> "No pressure at all. I'll send over [RELEVANT CONTENT — case study, guide, ROI calculator]. Take a look when you have time, and if you want to revisit this, I'm a call away."

## Red Flags (Disqualify Early)

🚩 "We just want a chatbot on our website" → Wrong fit. We build operators, not widgets.
🚩 "Can you do it for $500?" → Wrong budget tier. Point to DIY resources.
🚩 "We need this by tomorrow" → Unrealistic timeline. We ship in 1-2 weeks, not hours.
🚩 "I need to check with my team/partner/board" + no follow-up date → Polite no. Set a check-in or move on.
🚩 "What exactly is AI?" → Too early in their journey. Send educational content, revisit in 3 months.
