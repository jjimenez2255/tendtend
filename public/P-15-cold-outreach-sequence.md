# Cold Outreach Sequence — 5 Emails

*Day 0 → Day 3 → Day 7 → Day 14 → Day 21*
*Alfred personalizes and sends. Thomas reviews first 10 per batch, then auto-send.*

---

## Sequence Philosophy

1. **Every email earns the right to the next one.** No "just checking in." Each adds value.
2. **Personalize or don't send.** Alfred researches each prospect — their industry, team size, recent news, pain signals.
3. **Short > long.** Under 100 words ideally. Respect their inbox.
4. **One CTA per email.** Don't ask them to read an article AND book a call AND reply.
5. **Sound like a person, not a company.** First name sign-off. No corporate headers. No "Dear [NAME]."

---

## Email 1: The Opener (Day 0)

**Subject lines (test 3, kill 2):**
- A. `Quick question about [COMPANY]`
- B. `[NAME], saw [SPECIFIC THING] and had a thought`
- C. `The [INDUSTRY] lead problem`

**Body:**

```
Hey [NAME],

I was looking at [COMPANY] — [GENUINE OBSERVATION: "your Google reviews are solid," "saw you just opened a second location," "noticed you're hiring for an office manager"].

Quick question: how are you handling lead follow-up right now? Most [INDUSTRY] businesses we talk to lose 30-40% of inquiries because nobody gets back to them fast enough.

We build AI agents that respond to every lead in under 5 minutes — personalized, not a chatbot script — and handle follow-ups automatically.

Worth a 15-minute chat to see if it'd make sense for [COMPANY]?

— Thomas
Tend | tend.team
```

**Why it works:**
- Opens with something real about their business (not flattery — observation)
- Asks a question they can actually answer
- Gives a specific, relevant stat (30-40% lost leads)
- CTA is low commitment (15 minutes, "see if it makes sense")

## Email 3: The Insight (Day 7)

**Subject lines:**
- A. `[INDUSTRY] businesses are changing how they handle ops`
- B. `Something I noticed about [COMPANY]'s setup`
- C. `Quick observation — [NAME]`

**Body:**

```
[NAME],

One thing I keep seeing with [INDUSTRY] businesses your size: the owner is doing $20/hr work during $200/hr time.

Scheduling. Follow-ups. Confirming appointments. Updating spreadsheets. It all needs to happen, but it's not what grows the business.

The shift happening right now is simple: the repetitive 80% gets handled by an AI agent, and the team focuses on the 20% that actually moves the needle.

We do this for [INDUSTRY] companies in DFW. Setup is 1-2 weeks, and most clients see the difference in the first 48 hours.

If you've got 15 minutes this week, I'd love to show you what it looks like.

[CAL LINK]

— Thomas
```

**Why it works:**
- "$20/hr work during $200/hr time" — hits them in the gut. Every business owner feels this.
- Frames the offer as a movement ("the shift happening now"), not a sales pitch
- First time including a calendar link — they've had two no-pressure emails first
- Specific timeframe ("1-2 weeks," "first 48 hours") builds confidence

## Email 4: The Breakup (Day 14)

**Subject lines:**
- A. `Closing the loop, [NAME]`
- B. `Not the right time?`
- C. `Last one from me`

**Body:**

```
Hey [NAME],

I've reached out a few times and haven't heard back — totally fine. Timing matters, and I don't want to be that person clogging your inbox.

I'll leave it here. If you ever want to explore how an AI agent could handle your [SPECIFIC PAIN — "lead follow-up" / "scheduling" / "admin load"], the door's open.

In the meantime, here's something genuinely useful with no strings: [LINK TO P-24 GUIDE or P-23 SCORECARD if live, otherwise omit this line].

All the best with [COMPANY].

— Thomas
```

**Why it works:**
- Respectful. Acknowledges the silence without guilt.
- "That person clogging your inbox" — self-aware humor
- Leaves the door open without begging
- Optional value-add (guide/scorecard) — gives even if they never respond
- This email often gets the HIGHEST reply rate. People respect someone who knows when to stop.

## Email 5: The Re-engage (Day 21)

**Subject line:**
- `One more thing, [NAME]`

**Body:**

```
[NAME],

I said I'd leave it — and I will after this. But I saw [TIMELY TRIGGER: "you posted about hiring an office manager," "your competitor just launched automated booking," "[INDUSTRY] article about AI adoption"] and thought of our conversation.

If the timing is ever right: [CAL LINK]

Rooting for [COMPANY] either way.

— Thomas
```

**Why it works:**
- Only send if there's a genuine trigger. If there's no trigger, skip this email entirely.
- "I said I'd leave it" — honest callback, builds trust
- "Rooting for you either way" — zero pressure, genuine warmth
- This is the email that catches people 3-6 months later when their situation changes

## Sequence Rules for Alfred

### Sending
- Max 50 new contacts/day (start at 20, ramp over 2 weeks)
- Send Monday-Thursday, 8:00-10:00 AM CT (highest open rates)
- Never send Friday PM or weekends
- Space follow-ups exactly as scheduled (Day 0, 3, 7, 14, 21)

### Personalization Requirements
| Email | Minimum Personalization |
|-------|----------------------|
| #1 | Company observation + industry-specific pain point |
| #2 | Similar company reference (same industry or size) |
| #3 | Industry-specific insight + specific pain point |
| #4 | Reference specific capability from their context |
| #5 | Timely trigger or skip entirely |

**If Alfred can't find personalization material → skip that prospect.** A generic email damages the brand more than no email.

### Stop Conditions
- **Reply received** → Exit sequence immediately, route to Thomas
- **Out-of-office** → Pause sequence, resume 3 days after return date
- **Bounce** → Remove from list, mark email invalid
- **Unsubscribe request** → Remove immediately, log for compliance
- **"Not interested" reply** → Respectful acknowledgment, move to nurture list (revisit in 90 days)

### Tracking
For each sequence run, Alfred tracks:
- Open rate per email (benchmark: 35-50% for #1, declining to 20-30% by #4)
- Reply rate per email (benchmark: 2-5% for #1, often spikes on #4)
- Positive reply rate (benchmark: 1-3% = good, 3-5% = great)
- Booked calls from sequence (ultimate metric)
- Best-performing subject line per position

---

## Variant Testing

Alfred runs A/B tests on subject lines:
- Each email position has 2-3 subject line variants
- Split evenly across prospects
- After 50 sends per variant, declare winner
- Winner becomes default, loser gets replaced with new test
- Monthly: review overall sequence performance, rewrite underperformers

---

## Post-Reply Handling

**Positive reply ("Sure, let's talk" / "Tell me more"):**
1. Alfred drafts warm response + Cal.com link
2. Thomas reviews before send (first month) → auto-send after trust builds
3. Pipeline: move to "Discovery Scheduled"

**Soft positive ("Not right now but maybe later"):**
1. Alfred responds: "Totally understand. I'll check back in [30/60/90] days. In the meantime, [relevant resource]."
2. Pipeline: move to "Nurture — Revisit [DATE]"
3. Set reminder in pipeline

**Negative ("Not interested" / "Remove me"):**
1. Alfred responds: "No problem. Removed you from our outreach. All the best."
2. Remove from all active sequences
3. Log response for compliance

**Hostile ("Stop emailing me" / angry):**
1. Alfred responds: "Apologies for the inconvenience. You've been removed. Won't hear from us again."
2. Immediate removal + blocklist
3. Flag to Thomas (Tier 1 notification)
